SPOOFING

What is spoofing? What different types of spoofing are there?

Spoofing is the disguising of malicious material as trusted materials and data. The most common of which is IP-Spoofing, which is where Hackers replace their IP address with a legitimate IP address which computers trust, and sends packets to these users as ‘trusted packets’ and allowing for ensuing hacks to occur. The second example of spoofing is called ARP-Spoofing, which is when a hacker maps their IP address to a company's Media Access Control (MAC) address, which allows hackers to intercept packets being sent to MAC addresses, stealing potentially valuable and confidential data The third type of Spoofing is DNS-Spoofing, which is is when a hacker redirects a domain name of a trusted website, or a website to a Legitimate-looking site that further spreads other virus software like worms or malware.
